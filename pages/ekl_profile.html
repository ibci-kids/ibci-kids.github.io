Lab Description

At the BCI4Kids lab in the BME Program, we aim to translate the evolving brain-computer interface space for children. We are focused on the multitude of digital engineering problems facing BCI translation to children: from digital signal processing, machine learning, and analysis of neurophysiological signals, to the development of engaging, exciting applications for children to play with BCI systems.

Research Interest Keywords

BCI gaming, user-centered design, user-experience/user interaction design, digital signal processing, multimodal/multi-way analysis, contrastive machine learning,

Current research topics

  • Creating engaging applications and games for BCI systems
  • Structural and functional imaging of children and adults with quadriplegic cerebral palsy (qCP)
  • Developing engineering solutions for translating implantable BCIs to children with complex needs
